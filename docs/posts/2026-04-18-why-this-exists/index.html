Why this exists

A short intro to what this site is, who it's for, and what I'm trying to prove.

I start as Senior Director, AI Programs at CodePath on April 20, 2026. Two days before starting, I built the system that generated this page.

That timing isn't an accident. CodePath's internal bet is that AI tools are reshaping what a knowledge worker can do — and the only honest way to understand that claim is to live inside the tooling yourself. Before I walk into my first 1:1 with Chris, I want real reps using Cowork, building a persistent knowledge system, and learning the daily rhythm of ingesting and recalling information. Not as a theory — as a practice.

This site is the public face of that practice. My private repo holds the actual notes: onboarding prep, organizational context, partnership briefs, daily reflections. The public repo holds two things only: aggregate metrics about how the system is growing, and posts like this one where I explain how I'm using it or what I'm learning.

If you're reading this because you're thinking about building something similar, a few things worth knowing:

  • The setup isn't complicated. It's a collection of markdown files, a few Python and bash scripts, and Eleventy wired to GitHub Pages. You could recreate it over a weekend.
  • The hard part isn't the tooling. It's the discipline of actually writing things down — and building habits around asking the AI to help you recall, not just produce.
  • The site lags my real brain by a day. Everything rebuilds nightly on a cron.

If you work at CodePath and want to build your own, reach out. I'd rather help you set up your own than demo mine.
